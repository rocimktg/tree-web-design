Q: What plans do you offer?

We offer three website plans with no setup fees.

Lite ($149/mo, month-to-month): A focused one-page conversion site with hosting, SSL, updates, click-to-call, service-area wording, a Review Request Page, and one-time Google Profile setup.

Growth ($349/mo, 6-month agreement): Everything in Lite plus 5 core pages, 3 service-area pages, monthly Google Profile Management, 1 focused Google Search campaign, and up to 1 new or improved service / service-area page per month.

Pro ($549/mo, 12-month agreement): Everything in Growth plus 10 service-area pages, weekly Google Profile Management, 3–5 focused Google Search campaigns, and Local Services Ads support where available. Ad spend is separate.

All plans include hosting, SSL, website updates for text, photos, and content changes, and a $0 setup fee.

Q: How many pages do I get?

Lite includes a focused one-page conversion site. It is built to cover the essentials clearly: your services, service area, photos, trust signals, reviews, contact form, and click-to-call.

Growth includes 5 core pages plus 3 service-area pages, then up to 1 new or improved service / service-area page per month.

Pro includes 5 core pages plus 10 service-area pages, then up to 2 new or improved service / service-area pages per month.

Additional pages are $100 each , one-time.

Ongoing page work may include a new service page, a new service-area page, or improvements to an existing page based on what makes the most sense for your business. Unused page work does not roll over.

Q: How does billing work?

There are no setup fees on any plan.

Your first monthly payment is due at kickoff to start the build. Lite is month-to-month , Growth has a 6-month agreement , and Pro has a 12-month agreement .

Growth and Pro continue month-to-month after the initial term unless you cancel, change plans, or choose a buyout option.

Recurring payments are handled automatically through Stripe on the same billing date each month.

Q: What do I own on my website plan?

Your domain, logo, photos, and written copy are yours.

The live website runs on Tree Web Design's managed hosting while your monthly plan is active. As long as you stay on the plan, your site stays live and everything continues as normal.

Q: Can I buy out my website?

Yes, on Growth and Pro . After your initial term, the buyout is $0 — you just need to choose the buyout option and the finished website files are transferred to you.

From there, you can host the site wherever you want, or keep it hosted with Tree Web Design for $25/month .

Q: What happens after my initial term?

The plan continues month-to-month. Nothing changes — your site stays live, edits continue, and all included services continue.

If you want to stop, you can choose the buyout option or let the managed site come offline.

Q: What happens if I cancel?

You can cancel before your next billing date.

If you cancel and do not choose buyout, the managed website comes offline at the end of your billing period.

No website files are transferred without buyout. Lite does not include a buyout option.

Your domain, logo, photos, and written copy remain yours.

Q: What is the Review Request Page?

Every plan includes a Review Request Page at your domain, such as yourbusiness.com/feedback.

You send customers the link or a QR code after the job.

Happy customers can leave a public review on Google, Yelp, Nextdoor, or other platforms.

If something was off, they can contact you privately first. See Demo.

Q: What do you need from me to get started?

Not much. Once you're ready to move forward, we'll need your logo, a few photos of your work, and basic business info — services, service area, contact details. If you don't have professional photos yet, we'll work with what you have. We handle everything else from there.

Q: Can you write the copy for my site?

Yes. You don't need to write a word. We'll pull what we need from our kickoff call — your services, your market, what makes you different — and write copy that sounds like a real tree service pro, not a template.

Q: How fast will my site launch?

Once we have your payment and materials — logo, business info, photos — we get to work. Most sites are live within 7–10 business days.

Q: Do I have to commit before seeing the work?

No. Before you pay anything, you'll see a working homepage built around your business — your services, your market, your name on it. Not a mockup or a template with your logo swapped in. A real, hand-coded page.

If it doesn't look right or feel like a fit, you walk away with nothing owed. Most people are ready to move forward after seeing it.

Q: Why don't you use WordPress or page builders?

WordPress sites rely on plugins that break and create security risks. Page builders bloat your code and slow your site down. We hand-code everything from scratch. The result is a site that loads in under a second, never breaks on its own, and has nothing for hackers to exploit.

Q: Do you manage Google Profiles?

Yes. Google Profile support is built into all three plans. Google Search Ads support is included with Growth and Pro.

Lite includes one-time setup to make sure your profile is complete and aligned with your website.

Growth includes monthly Google Profile Management and 1 focused Google Search campaign.

Pro includes active weekly Google Profile Management, 3–5 focused Google Search campaigns, and Local Services Ads setup/support where available. Ad spend is separate.

Q: Is ad spend included?

No. Google Ads and Local Services Ads spend is paid directly to Google and is not included in the monthly plan.

The plan includes setup, tracking, landing page direction, and light monthly optimization where listed.

Q: Can every tree service company run Local Services Ads?

Not always. Local Services Ads availability, approval requirements, and lead volume vary by location and business category.

Pro includes setup/support where LSA is available and makes sense.

Q: What does ongoing page work mean?

It means we can add or improve service, service-area, or specialty pages over time based on what people are searching for and what your business wants to target.

Some months that may mean a new page. Other months it may mean improving an existing page.

Growth includes up to one per month. Pro includes up to two per month.

Unused ongoing page work does not roll over.

Q: What do website updates include?

Unlimited website updates means reasonable text, photo, and content changes to existing pages. It does not include new pages, new major sections, custom features, full redesigns, or ongoing SEO page expansion unless those are included in your plan.

Lite includes website updates for reasonable text, photo, and content changes to the existing one-page site. New service pages, service-area pages, specialty pages, new sections, or larger layout changes may be quoted separately unless included in your plan.

Q: Do tree service companies really need a website?

Yes. Tree work is high-trust, high-ticket, and often urgent. Homeowners compare companies quickly, especially on mobile. A fast, professional website helps you look credible, explain your services, and make it easy to call.

Q: How do I rank my tree service on Google Maps?

Google Maps ranking comes down to three things: a complete and active Google Business Profile, a fast website, and consistent five-star reviews.

Your Google Business Profile needs accurate service categories, real job photos, and regular updates. Your website needs to load fast, mention your city and services clearly, and link to your Google Business Profile. Reviews are the multiplier — the more recent and relevant, the higher you climb.

Lite includes one-time profile setup. Growth includes monthly Google Profile Management. Pro includes active weekly Google Profile Management — we keep your profile current with photos and posts from jobs you send us.

Q: Is Lite just a single-page website?

Lite is a one-page conversion site for tree companies that need a professional website without a larger SEO buildout. It covers the essentials: services, service area, trust signals, reviews, contact form, and click-to-call. If you want dedicated service pages, service-area pages, and ongoing local search expansion, Growth is the better fit.

Q: Which plan is right for my tree service company?

Lite is best for a professional online foundation. Growth is best for stronger local search support, service pages, service-area coverage, and one Search campaign. Pro is best for broader market coverage, more paid search support, and expansion across multiple nearby service areas.

Search Ads + Local Services Ads

## Search & Local Services Ads

Both can work well for tree companies, but they do different jobs.

## Google Search Ads

Search Ads are keyword-based ads that target specific services like tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, and emergency work.

These campaigns usually involve:

- Keyword targeting

- Negative keywords

- Location targeting

- Ad copy

- Landing pages

- Call-focused tracking

- Budget management

- Monthly review and adjustments

They give you more control over keywords, pages, messaging, and service focus.

## Local Services Ads

Local Services Ads are Google's local lead format built around reviews, service areas, and direct lead actions.

LSAs are especially helpful when your company has:

- Strong Google reviews

- A complete Google Business Profile

- Fast response time

- Clear service areas

- Proper business documentation

- A real process for handling calls and leads

LSAs can work well, but weak categories, missed calls, slow responses, or incomplete profiles can waste money.

Which Ad Path Fits Your Tree Company?

## Not Every Tree Company Should Start With the Same Ad Setup

Google Search Ads are usually the better starting point if you want more control over keywords, services, service areas, landing pages, and budget. They are also a practical option if Local Services Ads are not available yet, your profile is still being built, or you want to start with a more flexible campaign.

Local Services Ads may be a better fit if your company already has strong reviews, a complete Google Business Profile, clear service areas, and a fast response process. LSAs can be powerful, but they usually take more setup, verification, and follow-up discipline.

Sometimes the right move is to fix the foundation first. If your website is weak, your reviews are thin, your Google profile is incomplete, or calls are regularly missed, ads may expose those problems instead of solving them.

The goal is simple: choose the ad path that fits where your business is right now.

Built Around Calls

The Goal Is Not Traffic.
