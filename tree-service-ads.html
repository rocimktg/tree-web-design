Search Ads + Local Services Ads

Search & Local Services Ads

Google Search Ads and Local Services Ads can both be useful, but they are not the same thing.

Google Search Ads

Google Search Ads are the traditional text ads that appear when someone searches for specific keywords. They can target services like tree removal, trimming, stump grinding, emergency tree service, lot clearing, and other high-value work.

These campaigns usually involve:

  • Keyword targeting
  • Negative keywords
  • Location targeting
  • Ad copy
  • Landing pages
  • Call-focused tracking
  • Budget management
  • Monthly review and adjustments

Search Ads give you more control over keywords, pages, messaging, and service focus.

Local Services Ads

Local Services Ads are Google's local lead-focused ads that appear in a different format than regular search ads. For tree companies, LSAs are built around local trust signals, service areas, reviews, and direct lead actions.

LSAs are especially helpful when your company has:

  • Strong Google reviews
  • A complete Google Business Profile
  • Fast response time
  • Clear service areas
  • Proper business documentation
  • A real process for handling calls and leads

LSAs can be powerful, but they still need to be watched. Bad-fit leads, weak categories, missed calls, slow responses, or incomplete profiles can waste money.

Which Ad Path Fits Your Tree Company?

Not Every Tree Company Should Start With the Same Ad Setup

Google Search Ads are usually the better starting point if you want more control over keywords, services, cities, landing pages, and budget. They are also a practical option if Local Services Ads are not available yet, your profile is still being built, or you want to start with a more flexible campaign.

Local Services Ads may be a better fit if your company already has strong reviews, a complete Google Business Profile, clear service areas, and a fast response process. LSAs can be powerful, but they usually take more setup, verification, and follow-up discipline.

Sometimes the right move is to fix the foundation first. If your website is weak, your reviews are thin, your Google profile is incomplete, or calls are regularly missed, ads may expose those problems instead of solving them.

The goal is simple: choose the ad path that fits where your business is right now.

Questions

Tree Service Ads FAQ

Common questions about Google Ads and Local Services Ads for tree companies.

Are Google Ads and Local Services Ads the same thing?

No. Google Search Ads are keyword-based ads that usually send people to your website or landing page. Local Services Ads are a different Google ad format built more around local trust, reviews, service areas, and direct leads.

Both can be useful, but they need different setup and management.

Is ad spend included in the monthly plan?

No. Ad spend is paid directly to Google and is not included in the monthly plan.

The monthly plan covers support, setup, review, management, and website alignment depending on your plan.

Do I need a website to run ads?

Technically, not always. Practically, yes, you should have one.

A strong website gives people a place to verify your company, view your services, see proof, understand your service area, and contact you. Ads work better when the website builds trust.

How much should a tree company spend on ads?

It depends on your market, competition, service area, and goals.

A small test budget can show early signals, but competitive tree service markets usually need enough budget to collect data and make adjustments. If the budget is too small, it may be hard to learn what is working.

Can ads help me get emergency tree service calls?

Yes, emergency tree service can be a strong ad category because people often search when they need help quickly.

The campaign needs to be set up carefully so you are not paying for bad-fit searches, out-of-area leads, or calls you cannot answer.

Do LSAs work for tree companies?

They can, especially when the company has strong reviews, a complete Google profile, good response habits, and a clear service area.

LSAs still need monitoring. Missed calls, bad-fit leads, weak profile details, or poor follow-up can hurt performance.

Will ads help my SEO?

Ads do not directly replace SEO.

The better way to think about it: SEO builds long-term visibility. Ads help you show up faster while that foundation grows. A strong website, service pages, reviews, and Google Business Profile support both.
