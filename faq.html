Straight Answers.

Everything tree service pros ask before signing on.

What plans do you offer?

Three plans. No setup fees.

Lite ($75/mo, month-to-month): A fast, professional single-page website. Built custom, hosted, and ready to go. Lite includes operational update requests (business hours, phone number, address changes, etc.) plus one annual site refresh. For unlimited edits with no per-change fees, upgrade to Growth.

Growth ($175/mo, 6-month agreement): Everything in Lite plus the Review Request Page, unlimited text and photo edits, and one-time Google Business Profile setup. This is where most tree service pros land.

Pro ($275/mo, 12-month agreement): Everything in Growth plus active monthly Google Business Profile management, a review generation system, and 10 custom service area pages built to rank in every neighborhood you serve.

All plans include secure hosting, SSL, and a $0 setup fee.

How many pages do I get?

Lite includes 1 page. Growth includes up to 5 pages — Home, Services, About, Service Area, and Contact. Pro includes 5 core pages and 10 service area pages (15 total). Additional pages on any plan are $100 each, one-time.

What is the Review Request Page?

The Review Request Page is a simple feedback page built into every Growth and Pro site — accessible at your domain (e.g., yourbusiness.com/feedback). When a job is done, you send the customer a link or they scan a QR code from your business card or truck wrap. They land on a clean, mobile-friendly page where they can leave a review on Google, Yelp, Nextdoor, or other platforms. It also gives customers a private channel to reach you directly if something wasn't right — so you can fix it before it becomes a public one-star review. See Demo.

Do I own my website?

Your domain, logo, photos, and written copy are yours permanently — we hand all of that back within 5 business days if you ever leave. The website itself runs on our infrastructure, which means if you stop paying, the site comes down. No exceptions, no grace period extensions. If you want to keep a web presence after canceling, we'll help you transition your content to a new provider.

What happens if I cancel?

Your site comes down at the end of your billing period. We deliver your domain, photos, and copy within 5 business days of cancellation. The site runs on our infrastructure, so it cannot remain live after your plan ends.

How does billing work?

There are no setup fees on any plan. Your first monthly payment is due at project kickoff to start the build — there are no additional onboarding or activation costs. Recurring payments are handled automatically via Stripe on the same day each month. Your rate is locked in for the duration of your initial term.

What do you need from me to get started?

Not much. Once you're ready to move forward, we'll need your logo, a few photos of your work, and basic business info — services, service area, contact details. If you don't have professional photos yet, we'll work with what you have. We handle everything else from there.

Can you write the copy for my site?

Yes. You don't need to write a word. We'll pull what we need from our kickoff call — your services, your market, what makes you different — and write copy that sounds like a real tree service pro, not a template.

How fast will my site launch?

Once we have your payment and materials — logo, business info, photos — we get to work. Most sites are live within 7–10 business days.

Can I add more pages?

Yes. Additional pages are $100 each, one-time, on any plan. No package upgrade required — just let us know what you need and we'll add it.

Why don't you use WordPress or page builders?

WordPress sites rely on plugins that break and create security risks. Page builders bloat your code and slow your site down. We hand-code everything from scratch. The result is a site that loads in under a second, never breaks on its own, and has nothing for hackers to exploit.

Do you manage Google Business profiles?

Yes. It's included in the Pro plan — we start with a full audit to make sure your profile is complete and accurate, then keep it active by adding photos and updates from jobs you send us via text or email. A complete, active profile ranks higher in local search and turns more map views into calls. Growth plan clients receive a one-time Google Business Profile setup at launch.

Do you work with businesses outside of tree service?

Yes. While tree service is our specialty, we work with home service pros across the trades — roofing, landscaping, electrical, and more. The same approach applies: fast, hand-coded sites built to convert visitors into calls.

Do tree service companies really need a website?

Yes — and a proper tree service website matters more for tree service than most trades. Tree work is high-ticket and high-trust. Homeowners aren't just hiring the cheapest option; they're hiring whoever looks most credible when they search. A slow or outdated site — or no site at all — hands those jobs to whoever shows up next in Google.

Beyond credibility, a well-built site works for you 24/7. It captures leads after hours, during storms, and on weekends when your phone is off. The tree service pros getting the most calls aren't always the best in their market. They're just the most visible online.

How do I rank my tree service on Google Maps?

Google Maps ranking comes down to three things: a complete and active Google Business Profile, a fast website, and consistent five-star reviews.

Your Google Business Profile needs accurate service categories, real job photos, and regular updates. Your website needs to load fast, mention your city and services clearly, and link to your Google Business Profile. Reviews are the multiplier — the more recent and relevant, the higher you climb.

Growth plan clients get a one-time Google Business Profile setup at launch. Pro plan clients get active monthly Google Business Profile management — we keep your profile current with photos and posts from jobs you send us.

What features does every arborist website need?

At minimum: a mobile-first design (most searches happen on phones), a click-to-call button on every page, a fast contact or estimate form, and real photos from your jobs. Those are table stakes.

The features that actually move the needle for tree companies are a Review Request Page to collect Google reviews after every job, and service area pages targeting the specific cities you work in. Every plan we build includes the basics. Growth and Pro add the rest.
